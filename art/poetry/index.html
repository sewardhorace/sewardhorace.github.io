a cosmic paperweight

I am faker than a plastic rake cracked by autumn cold  faker than all plastic  faker than the industrial age  faker than a cardboard fast-food crown  faker than a bleary-eyed drunken smile  faker than a hardwood floor  faker than whole-wheat pancake mix faker than a chicken nugget  faker than an ad for cologne.  I am the fakest motherfucker  since the moss mountain shed its green and became a bald rock face of lies and crumbling.  I am the backdrop that flies in for the broadway showstopper,       drawn by a kindergarten class after naptime.  I am a leopard print dress in line for the club.  I am salt and hotsauce.  I am fingerless gloves in mild weather.  I am deep lines on the forehead of       a sixty five year old tea guru.  I am the cameraman behind the endzone.  I am a blackboard eraser never washed.  I am the plunger on the cartoon TNT box.  My stomach is the heaviest thing.  My feet are sandstone sculptures,  My hands are poison ivy.  My arms are rusted railroad spikes.  My chest is a paper bag filled with splinters.  My face is wild horses running in the street.  My legs are a paper airplane.  I am faker than Mt. Olympus.  I am in a fluorescent stupor,       still drowsy upon waking.  My eyes are sauteed in the ocean.  I crouch, waiting for the sunset.  In my sleep I will leap into the atmosphere with a flat rate box       and I will gather space dust            and I will become irradiated.  My cosmic powers are infinite and I am wiser than dreamtime legends.  I am faker than moss mountain unfurling her loom legs and weaving me a blanket       from the dusk dust.  Her dog curls beneath her low loom music.  The dog is uncertain, insubstantial.  He barks on 2 and 4 and I am pleased.  I mail him my box for a low low price       but he has no blade to open it            and so uses it to weigh down his doctoral thesis                 on incidental mass                      which will never be published                           or even read.

Blue Sky Blood

dead leaves degrade,  shrivel like the eye sockets of a cow corpse out to pasture,  crumble and flake between dirt.  My beard of dead leaves drips with sky blood,  the thick syrup of starlight and space, azure with dream pollution,  which at once delays and insures my own slow decay.  I wore wide-brimmed hats to catch the drops like rain  but I don't like the way I look in hats  so my eyes will forever flick skyward and watch the dawn of each day  soak into my beard  as the skin of my face shrinks and swells like leather.

trapped in a dumb sandwich

Your jaw is more angular than your glasses  you speak thin-lipped at the room for twenty-six unbroken minutes  "establish a rapport," and, "allow yourself to stray from the priority questions."  I cannot see into their heads or my own - only the outside.  I want to go out  If I leave I do not want to go anywhere  sit in a different room with no walls where everyone is saying  nothing. no,  someone is saying  everything. no,  nobody is saying  everything. no,  nobody is saying  something. no,  anybody is saying  something and I am writing and  I am listening and I am anybody

Nightwalk

On a nightwalk  alone  to her I have spoke the ultimate thursday  "Your knuckles crack like an avalanche,  your shirt is piano keys and it is buildings."  have a fever and bite granite fingernails  walk through ink puddles without ripples  GONE TO EAT THE SUN  BACK IN 15 MINS.

Jan 16

This is a strange day.  I yawn and fleas run down my chin.  I perpetually have the feeling of a full bladder and an empty stomach.  My skin is falling off in continent-sized chunks.  I speak Japanese like a snail, whorling in my beige shell.  I present tense moments of abstract   none sense:  Doubt crawls dying and bleeding across the floor of the classroom.  Students scooch their chairs away to avoid his wavering grasps.  Gasping silently, he puckers his lips to suck in air.  An eyeball rolls out of his face and bounces twice on the linoleum.   "Clean this up."  There are no janitors employed here qualified to clean up this self-reflective leaking pile.  Greasy and grey, stains too tough for hand soap and paper towels tear like tissue.  A thick torrent of seawater explodes into the room through the door gape, ruining electronic equipment and costing the school thousands.  I smell the salt and I am reminded of my grandfather, sitting pale in his soft chair, his soft chest heaving with effort, body laced with plaque and other substances that meet well with the tongue.  He watches westerns until they are all the same, sometimes with the sound off.  Hands folded over his stomach and a subtle scowl that his complacent eyes cover like a white sheet.   Dastardly thoughts whip me back into the room, where pencils are shouting graphite in a mob.  Reins crack and teeth are gnashing in the mouths of these sleeping young horses.  Nine times the base weight of lead, the heaviest yet, aluminum in its texture, denial hovers over heads like a guillotine, softly whispering to plugged ears, and downcast eyes could never catch a glimpse.  I think for a moment about flipping a chair, so some upturned heads could glance beyond their blinders.  But their heads are wrapped in DNA spliced from cigarette butts on damp asphalt and shiny plastic shoes squeaking cleanly against piled skulls in the graveyard down the block.  Ivy weaves through the body, pleasantly obscuring.   It has been cloudy for weeks.
